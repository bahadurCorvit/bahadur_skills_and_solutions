3. Scanning Networks

Port scanning, vulnerability detection, and network mapping.

SQL injection, XSS, and web security vulnerabilities.

Wi-Fi security attacks and protection methods.

11. Ethical Hacking Lab Practice

Real-world penetration testing labs using Kali Linux and cybersecurity tools.

Multiple choice questions covering ethical hacking concepts, tools, and real-world security scenarios.

CEH certification is valid globally and highly recognized in cybersecurity industry.

📚 Eligibility Requirements

Basic understanding of networking, IT fundamentals, or completion of CEH training is recommended.

CEH Exam Preparation Strategy (Step-by-Step Passing Guide 2026)

Passing the CEH exam requires a smart study strategy, consistent practice, and hands-on experience with cybersecurity tools. Below is a complete step-by-step preparation roadmap for beginners and intermediate learners.

📚 Step-by-Step CEH Preparation Plan

Step 1: Understand Networking Fundamentals (IP, DNS, OSI Model)
Step 2: Learn Cybersecurity Basics (Threats, Attacks, Defense)
Step 3: Practice Ethical Hacking Tools (Nmap, Metasploit, Wireshark)
Step 4: Work on Hands-on Labs (Kali Linux + Virtual Environments)
Step 5: Solve Mock Exams & Practice Questions Daily

🧠 Expert CEH Exam Tips

  • Focus on understanding concepts, not just memorizing
  • Practice subnetting and network scenarios daily
  • Spend time on real-world hacking labs
  • Use online mock tests before final exam
  • Revise OWASP Top 10 security vulnerabilities

⚠️ Common Mistakes Students Make

❌ Only watching videos without practice
❌ Ignoring networking fundamentals
❌ Not practicing tools regularly
❌ Last-minute exam preparation

Quick Answer:

To pass CEH exam, you need a structured study plan including networking basics, cybersecurity concepts, hands-on labs, and regular mock exam practice.
